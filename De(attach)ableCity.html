De(attach)able City

Gentrification and the displacement of the urban poor is a major contributor to social injustice in Johannesburg city. This system aims to transform the way Johannesburg’s urban environment deals with displaced people. It attempts to integrate the living conditions of displaced people in Johannesburg with abandoned buildings, whilst treating a long-standing urban challenge with humility.

Symbiosis - Buildings and the City
Abandoned, or buildings being redeveloped have been highlighted for housing the homeless. These buildings have fed off the city and continue to do so without giving back a parasitic relationship. By assisting in overcoming social injustices in the city, much like housing the homeless, buildings become advantageous to the city, a mutualistic relationship.

The Gold Star Rating System
In the broader spectrum, this proposal introduces a socially based rating system. Like the green star rating, the gold star rating will create a common measurement, a list of criteria that drives buildings to assist homeless, informal traders and the general public.
